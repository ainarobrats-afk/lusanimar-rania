# Immigration

This guide explains arrival and departure immigration procedures.

## Arrival immigration

- Follow signs for arrivals, passport control, or immigration.
- Prepare passport, visa, and arrival card.
- Fill out arrival forms clearly and completely.
- Present your documents to the immigration officer.
- Answer questions honestly and politely.
- Wait for your passport to be stamped before proceeding.

## Departure immigration

- Follow signs for departures and immigration.
- Have passport and boarding pass ready.
- Some airports require you to show onward or return tickets.
- Be prepared for passport checks and boarding pass review.

## Arrival card guidance

- Use block letters if writing by hand.
- Fill in name exactly as on passport.
- Enter passport number, nationality, flight number, and purpose of visit.
- Provide the address of your first stay in the destination country.
- Sign the form and write the date.

## E-gate and automated immigration

- E-gates are often available for citizens or eligible passports.
- Scan your passport and complete the biometric process.
- E-gates are usually faster than manual passport control.
- If the gate does not accept your passport, proceed to the regular queue.

## Tips

- Do not take photos inside immigration areas.
- Keep the arrival card until after immigration is complete.
- Check whether your destination requires a visa on arrival.
