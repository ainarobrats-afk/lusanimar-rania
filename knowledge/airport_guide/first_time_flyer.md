# First Time Flyer

This guide helps passengers who are flying for the first time.

## Before the trip

- Check your flight date, time, and terminal.
- Complete online check-in if available.
- Pack light and keep important documents in your carry-on.
- Make sure your passport is valid and you have any required visas.
- Bring a small travel bag with medicines, charger, and a change of clothes.

## Getting to the airport

- Arrive early: 3 hours before international flights and 90 minutes before domestic flights.
- Bring your boarding pass and passport.
- Know your airline and terminal.

## At the airport

- Find the airline counter or self check-in kiosk.
- If you have checked bags, drop them at the bag drop counter.
- Pass through security with boarding pass and passport ready.
- Follow airport signs to the departure gate.

## Boarding the plane

- Watch for boarding announcements and gate information.
- Most airlines board by group or row.
- Scan your boarding pass at the gate.
- Find your seat and store your carry-on above or under the seat.
- Fasten your seatbelt and listen to safety instructions.

## After landing

- Follow signs to immigration and baggage claim.
- Show your passport and arrival card to immigration.
- Collect your luggage and exit through customs.
- Look for transportation options to your destination.

## Tips for first timers

- Take notes on your flight number, gate, and terminal.
- Keep your passport in a safe but accessible place.
- Ask airport staff if you are unsure where to go.
- Stay calm and allow time for each step.
